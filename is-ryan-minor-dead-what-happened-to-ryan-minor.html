Is Ryan Minor Dead?

He received treatment, and in November 2023 he was placed in hospice care, but he eventually passed away from the illness. His passing signaled the end of an illustrious career in sports, coaching as well as playing.

What Happened To Ryan Minor?

After receiving a colon cancer diagnosis in late October 2022, Ryan Minor was faced with a difficult medical situation. He underwent treatments and fought the illness even after the diagnosis.

But in the end, the cancer proved to be an extremely strong foe, and in November 2023, he was admitted to hospice care. Due to his fight with colon cancer, Ryan Minor passed away on December 22, 2023, leaving a lasting legacy in the baseball and basketball communities.

Who Was Ryan Minor?

Born on January 5, 1974, Ryan Dale Minor is an American professional baseball player who played third base, managed minor league baseball, and also played professional basketball. From 1998 to 2001, he participated in four MLB seasons, playing for the Montreal Expos and the Baltimore Orioles.

In addition to playing baseball, Minor also played forward basketball and was selected by the Philadelphia 76ers in the 1996 NBA draft. Throughout his coaching career, he oversaw many Orioles teams and made a substantial impact on the game. Sadly, on December 22, 2023, he lost his fight with colon cancer at the age of 49.

Ryan Minor Early Life

Ryan Minor’s love of sports, especially baseball and basketball, defined his early years. He was born in Canton, Ohio, on January 5, 1974, and went to Hammon High School in Hammon, Oklahoma, where he was a standout baseball and basketball player. He was recognized for his abilities on the court and in the field, and in 1992 he was named to the ABCA/Rawlings High School All-America Baseball Third Team.

He had to decide between baseball and basketball, but at the University of Oklahoma, he played both. He went on to become an All-American basketball player in college, twice topping the Big Eight Conference in points scored per contest. He was also a college baseball player, helping the Oklahoma Sooners win the 1994 College World Series in his sophomore season.

Ryan Minor Career

Throughout his varied athletic career, Ryan Minor left his mark in both basketball and baseball. From 1998 to 2001, he was a Major League Baseball (MLB) third baseman for the Montreal Expos and the Baltimore Orioles. During his Major League debut in 1998, he notably took the place of the illustrious Cal Ripken Jr.

At the University of Oklahoma, Minor was a standout basketball player who won awards like Big Eight Player of the Year in 1995. His NBA career ended abruptly, even though the Philadelphia 76ers selected him in the first round of the 1996 NBA draft. As a result, he turned his attention to baseball.

Minor went from being a player to a coach, running several minor league teams. He helped shape young players’ careers and remained active in the game until he died in 2023.

Ryan Minor Cause of Death?

Colon cancer was the cause of Ryan Minor’s demise. He received treatment after receiving the diagnosis in late October 2022, and in November 2023 he was placed in hospice care.

At the age of 49, he lost his battle with colon cancer on December 22, 2023, despite his heroic efforts to fight the disease. With his passing, a strong athletic career and a life devoted to the love of the game came to an end.
