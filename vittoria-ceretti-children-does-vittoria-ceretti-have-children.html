Vittoria Ceretti is an Italian model who was discovered in 2012 through the Elite Model Look Model contest. Models.com ranks her among the “new super” of this generation.

Vittoria Ceretti was born in Brescia, Italy in 1998, the daughter of Giuseppe Ceretti, the owner of a flooring company, and Francesca (née Lazzari), a homemaker. At the age of 14, she entered the Elite Model Look competition in Italy and was selected as a finalist.

Vittoria Ceretti’s modeling career began when she was selected as a finalist in the Elite Model Look contest in Italy at the age of 14. With her beauty, big green eyes, and determination, Vittoria Ceretti has become one of the most recognizable faces in fashion.

Vittoria Ceretti made her debut on the runway in the Italian city of Milan, for designer Kristina Ti. Since then, she has been a model for Max Mara, Chloé, Paco Rabanne, Alexander McQueen, DKNY, Lanvin, Ralph Lauren, Alexander Wang, Jason Wu, Celine, Loewe, Jacquemus, Salvatore Ferragamo, Anna Sui, Hugo Boss, Dolce & Gabbana, Armani, Prada, Proenza Schouler, JW Anderson, Moschino, Fendi, Valentino, Roberto Cavalli, Versace, Missoni, Louis Vuitton, Burberry, Chanel, Christian Dior, Miu Miu, Givenchy, Alberta Ferretti, Marc Jacobs, Michael Kors, Bottega Veneta, Tom Ford, Tommy Hilfiger, Versace, and Yves Saint Laurent.

Ceretti appeared on the cover of the magazines Vogue, Vogue Italia, Vogue Paris, Vogue Japan, Vogue Germany, Vogue Spain, British Vogue, Vogue Korea, Vogue China, Harper’s Bazaar, Elle, Glamour, Grazia, IO Donna and more.

Vittoria Ceretti was one of seven models on the cover of Vogue’s March 2017 issue, celebrating the magazine’s 125th anniversary. According to Vogue Italia, Vittoria Ceretti was the most searched model of 2018 on their website.

In an interview for Vogue Paris, Vittoria Ceretti said that she would have studied acting or psychology if she hadn’t started a modeling career. She married Matteo Milleri, an Italian DJ, on June 1, 2020, in Ibiza, Spain.
