Who is Steph Curry real father?

The real father of Steph Curry is Dell Curry. Dell Curry is a former basketball player in the United States of America. From 1986 to 2002, he participated in the National Basketball Association (NBA), retiring as the Charlotte Hornets’ all-time leader in points (9,839) and made three-point field goals (929).

Currently, Dell Curry and Eric Collins serve as color commentators for Charlotte Hornets television broadcasts.

He was raised in Grottoes, Virginia, and played high school basketball at Fort Defiance. He used his coach’s barn to practice his shooting every day. He was born in Harrisonburg, Virginia. He was named a McDonald’s All-American in 1982 and finished as the school’s all-time leading scorer.

Dell Curry participated in both baseball and basketball, winning state championships in each. The Texas Rangers selected Curry in the first round of the 1982 Major League Baseball draft.

Who is Stephen Curry mom?

Stephen Curry’s mom is known as Sonya Alicia Curry. Cleive and Candy Adams welcomed her into the world in Radford, Virginia. They lived in utter poverty and were frequently racialized by the Ku Klux Klan.

In high school, she took part in basketball, track, and volleyball. She joined the student-athlete program at Virginia Tech, where she competed in volleyball and earned all-conference honors during her junior season in the Metro Conference. She received a degree in education.

She first met Dell Curry, the man she would eventually wed, at Virginia Tech. During her official recruiting visit to the university, Adams saw Curry while she was watching the men’s basketball practice.

She founded the Christian Montessori School of Lake Norman in Huntersville, North Carolina, and currently serves as its president. Her kids went to that school.
